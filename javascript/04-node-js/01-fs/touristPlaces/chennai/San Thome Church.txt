The San Thome Church in Chennai, tracing its roots to A.D. 52, is a symbol of colonial legacy. Originally built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later reconstructed by the British in 1893, this Roman Catholic basilica stands tall at 155 feet. Its towering spires overlook the tomb of St. Thomas, offering a serene atmosphere and architectural splendor against the backdrop of the sea, attracting pilgrims worldwide.
